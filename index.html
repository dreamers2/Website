Meet the Dreamer 

Dreamer 2 is a two-stage solid propellant sounding rocket with a payload capacity of 1 kg and capable of crossing the Karman Line following a parabolic trajectory.
Making use of a clean propellant and fully recoverable, Dreamer 2 has been designed for high performance and efficiency

Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, the 11th President of India

Why Dreamer 2?

One day, Bharat Ratna Prof. Kalam was sitting in his office keenly pondering over a bunch of drawings. When asked by Padma Bhushan Sri. Nambi Narayanan, the man behind the Vikas engine and the cryogenic propulsion technology, as to what he was doing, Prof. Kalam explained that he was trying to modify the RH family of rockets and had named his very own design - D1.
‘Why D1?’, asked Narayanan. “Have you completed A1, B1 and C1?”. Prof Kalam replied “D1 stands for Dreamer 1. I am not sure if this design would ever cruise into space”. He was right. D1 never lifted off the launch pad.
Almost forty years later, we have come together to put up Dreamer 2, with improved design and performance, as a tribute to the man who inspired millions.

What goes into Dreamer 2?
Structure 

An Aluminium Alloy 2050 structure coupled with a Ti-6Al-4V propellant chamber for better structural properties, capable of withstanding the extremes of spaceflight

Nose Cone

A Von Karman nose cone for better aerodynamics constructed of Aluminium Alloy 2050.
Enhances performance of the rocket and minimises fuel consumption significantly.

Stage Separator

A lightweight, tough and easy to manufacture fibreglass composite interstage connector with pyrobolts for easy stage separation with a provision to integrate advanced avionics and guidance systems.
Provides sufficient protection to the nozzle and insulates the electronics from excessive heat.

Fins

Trapezoidal Supersonic Fin built of Carbon Fibre Reinforced Polymer for enhanced performance by minimizing aerodynamic losses.
